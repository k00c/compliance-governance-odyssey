# Week 06: Incident Reporting and Investigation (Milestone 2)

## Overview

**Focus Area:** Incident Management, Investigation Methodology, Root Cause Analysis  
**Duration:** 1 Week  
**Estimated Time Commitment:** 18-22 hours  
**Milestone Project:** Complete Incident Investigation Case Study

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

1. Understand WA incident notification and reporting requirements
2. Apply ICAM (Incident Cause Analysis Method) or similar methodology
3. Conduct structured incident investigations
4. Perform root cause analysis to identify systemic factors
5. Distinguish between immediate, underlying, and root causes
6. Develop effective corrective and preventive actions
7. Write professional investigation reports
8. Complete **Milestone Project 2:** Full incident investigation package

## Milestone Project 2: Incident Investigation Case Study

### Overview

**Objective:** Complete a comprehensive incident investigation demonstrating professional investigation capability

**Estimated Effort:** 10-15 hours across Days 4-7

**Portfolio Value:** This milestone creates a complete investigation package that demonstrates systematic investigation methodology, root cause analysis, and professional documentation - highly valued by mining employers.

---

### Scenario

You are the Compliance Officer for **Goldfields Mining Pty Ltd**, a gold mining operation in WA. An incident has occurred requiring full investigation.

**Incident Summary:**
- **Date:** [Current date - 3 days]
- **Time:** 14:30 hours
- **Location:** Haul road between open pit and processing plant (2.3 km section)
- **Incident Type:** Light vehicle rollover
- **Vehicle:** Toyota LandCruiser 200 Series
- **Injuries:** Driver sustained minor bruising and neck strain, treated on-site by paramedic
- **Vehicle Damage:** Significant front and side damage, vehicle written off
- **Environmental Impact:** Minor hydraulic fluid spill (~10L), contained on road surface
- **Production Impact:** Haul road closed 4 hours for investigation and cleanup

**Initial Information:**
- Driver (age 35, 5 years site experience) was returning from pit inspection
- Incident occurred on straight section of road, clear visibility
- Weather conditions: Clear, dry, temperature 28Â°C
- Road conditions: Recently graded gravel surface, dry
- No other vehicles directly involved
- Speed limit: 60 km/h
- Estimated vehicle speed at incident: Unknown
- Driver's last fatigue assessment: Start of shift (7 hours prior)
- Vehicle pre-start completed: Yes, no issues noted
- Witness: Following vehicle ~200m behind saw incident

---

### Part 1: Investigation Planning (2 hours)

**Tasks:**
1. Develop investigation plan:
   - Investigation scope and objectives
   - Team composition (3-4 roles)
   - Timeline and key milestones
   - Resources required
   - Evidence preservation requirements
   - Communication protocols

2. Create evidence collection plan:
   - Scene examination requirements
   - Documents to review
   - People to interview
   - Physical evidence to collect
   - Expert resources needed

3. Prepare interview question guides for:
   - Driver (open-ended, non-leading questions)
   - Witness (vehicle behind)
   - Supervisor (context and background)
   - Paramedic (injury assessment)

**Deliverable:** Investigation Plan document

---

### Part 2: Evidence Collection and Documentation (3-4 hours)

**Tasks:**
1. Create hypothetical evidence package:
   
   **Documentary Evidence:**
   - Driver's training records (light vehicle, defensive driving, fatigue management)
   - Vehicle maintenance records (last 6 months)
   - Pre-start inspection form (day of incident)
   - Road inspection records (grading schedule, condition reports)
   - Speed limit signage location map
   - Driver's fatigue management records (roster, breaks)
   - Previous incidents on same road section
   - Site traffic management plan
   
   **Physical Evidence:**
   - Scene photographs (create descriptions): vehicle position, road condition, tire marks, debris field
   - Scene diagram showing: road layout, vehicle path, rollover sequence, final position
   - Vehicle damage assessment (describe damage pattern)
   - Road surface condition (describe gravel, corrugations, camber)
   
   **Testimonial Evidence:**
   - Create realistic interview summaries for:
     - Driver (what happened, contributing factors from their perspective)
     - Witness (what they observed)
     - Supervisor (driver's recent work history, any concerns)
     - Road maintenance coordinator (road condition, grading schedule)

2. Develop event timeline showing sequence from start of shift through incident

**Deliverable:** Evidence Package with scene diagram and documentation

---

### Part 3: Root Cause Analysis using ICAM (3-4 hours)

**Tasks:**
1. Apply ICAM methodology:
   
   **Event Analysis:**
   - Describe the incident event
   - Identify immediate consequences
   - Map event sequence
   
   **Conditional Analysis:**
   - Identify conditions present at the time (road condition, vehicle state, environmental factors, human factors)
   - Determine which conditions were necessary for incident to occur
   
   **Absent/Failed Defense Analysis:**
   - Identify what barriers/defenses should have prevented incident
   - Determine which defenses were absent or failed
   - Analyze why defenses failed
   
   **Causal Factor Analysis:**
   Identify contributing factors at each level:
   - **Individual factors:** (e.g., speed selection, situational awareness, fatigue)
   - **Task factors:** (e.g., time pressure, task complexity, conflicting priorities)
   - **Workplace factors:** (e.g., road design, vehicle condition, environmental conditions)
   - **Organizational factors:** (e.g., road maintenance frequency, speed monitoring, risk assessment, training adequacy)

2. Create ICAM chart showing causal relationships

3. Identify 2-3 root causes (organizational system failures)

**Deliverable:** ICAM Root Cause Analysis Report with chart

---

### Part 4: Investigation Report (3-4 hours)

**Tasks:**
1. Write formal investigation report with following structure:

   **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Incident overview
   - Key findings
   - Primary root causes
   - Critical recommendations

   **Introduction**
   - Investigation scope and objectives
   - Investigation team
   - Methodology used
   
   **Incident Description**
   - Factual account of what happened
   - Timeline of events
   - Immediate response actions
   
   **Investigation Methodology**
   - Evidence collection approach
   - Analysis methodology (ICAM)
   - Limitations or constraints
   
   **Findings**
   - Immediate causes
   - Contributing factors (individual, task, workplace, organizational)
   - Root causes (systemic organizational factors)
   - Failed or absent defenses
   
   **Recommendations** (8-10 recommendations)
   - Organized by hierarchy of controls
   - Each recommendation should:
     - Be specific and actionable
     - Address identified root causes
     - Be assigned to responsible party
     - Have target completion date
     - Include success criteria
   
   **Conclusion**
   - Overall assessment
   - Key learnings
   - Follow-up requirements
   
   **Appendices**
   - Scene photographs/diagrams
   - Interview summaries
   - ICAM chart
   - Evidence list

**Deliverable:** Formal Investigation Report (10-15 pages)

---

### Part 5: Supporting Documentation (2-3 hours)

**Tasks:**
1. **DMIRS Notification:**
   - Draft formal incident notification using DMIRS templates
   - Ensure all required information included
   - Appropriate timeframe and format
   - Professional communication tone

2. **Corrective Action Plan:**
   - Create action tracking spreadsheet with:
     - Action ID
     - Description
     - Root cause addressed
     - Responsible person
     - Target date
     - Status
     - Verification method
   - Prioritize actions (immediate, short-term, long-term)
   - Estimate resources required

3. **Safety Alert:**
   - One-page communication for site personnel
   - Describe incident (facts only)
   - Key learnings
   - Immediate actions required
   - Contact for questions

4. **Management Briefing:**
   - Two-page executive summary for senior management
   - Incident overview and impacts
   - Investigation findings (high-level)
   - Recommended actions and resource requirements
   - Implementation timeline

**Deliverables:**
- DMIRS notification draft
- Corrective action plan
- Safety alert
- Management briefing

## Additional Tips for Success

1. **Stay objective:** Investigations seek truth, not blame
2. **Be thorough:** Missing evidence can lead to wrong conclusions
3. **Think systemically:** Look for organizational factors, not just individual errors
4. **Document meticulously:** Your report may be used in legal proceedings
5. **Communicate clearly:** Findings must be understood by all audiences
6. **Focus on prevention:** Recommendations should prevent recurrence
7. **Respect participants:** Investigation can be stressful for those involved
8. **Learn continuously:** Each investigation builds your expertise
