## Capstone A: Compliance Management System Design

**Timeline:** Weeks 12-14 (after completing core weekly curriculum)  
**Estimated Effort:** 30-40 hours  
**Skill Integration:** Risk management, audit, regulatory frameworks, documentation, stakeholder engagement

### Executive Summary

Design a complete Compliance Management System (CMS) for a hypothetical WA mining company preparing for growth. This capstone demonstrates your ability to build compliance infrastructure from the ground up, a highly valued skill for organizations scaling their governance functions.

### Scenario

**Company:** Pilbara Gold Resources Ltd (hypothetical)  
**Profile:**
- Junior mining company transitioning to producer status
- Single mine site, 200 employees, growing to 400 within 18 months
- Recently secured funding for mine development
- Limited existing compliance infrastructure
- Board mandate to establish "best practice" compliance function

**Challenge:** Design a comprehensive CMS that will support the company through growth while meeting regulatory requirements and investor expectations.

### Project Components

#### Component 1: Compliance Framework Design (8-10 hours)

**1.1 Compliance Policy Suite**

Develop a hierarchy of compliance policies:

| Policy Level | Document | Purpose |
|--------------|----------|---------|
| Level 1 | Compliance Policy | Board-approved commitment to compliance |
| Level 2 | Compliance Management Standard | Framework and principles |
| Level 3 | Compliance Procedures | Operational procedures |
| Level 4 | Work Instructions | Specific task guidance |

**Deliverables:**
- Compliance Policy (2-3 pages, board-ready format)
- Compliance Management Standard (10-15 pages, detailed framework)
- Three sample procedures demonstrating procedure format

**1.2 Governance Structure**

Design the compliance governance structure:
- Compliance function organizational design
- Role descriptions (Compliance Manager, Compliance Officers)
- Reporting lines and escalation pathways
- Committee structure (Compliance Committee, Risk Committee)
- Board reporting requirements

**Deliverables:**
- Organizational chart with compliance function
- Role descriptions for 3 key compliance positions
- Committee terms of reference
- Board reporting framework

#### Component 2: Compliance Obligations Register (6-8 hours)

**2.1 Obligations Identification**

Create comprehensive obligations register covering:

| Category | Example Obligations |
|----------|---------------------|
| Mining Legislation | WA Mining Act, Mining Regulations |
| Environmental | EP Act, Clearing Permits, Part V licenses |
| Safety | Mines Safety Act, WHS Regulations |
| Corporate | Corporations Act, ASX Listing Rules |
| Employment | Fair Work Act, WA Industrial Relations |
| Financial | Taxation, Superannuation, Reporting |

**For each obligation document:**
- Obligation ID and description
- Source legislation/regulation
- Compliance requirements
- Evidence required
- Compliance owner
- Review frequency
- Consequences of non-compliance

**Deliverables:**
- Obligations register (minimum 50 obligations across categories)
- Obligations matrix by responsible party
- Critical obligations summary (top 20 by consequence)

**2.2 Compliance Calendar**

Develop 12-month compliance calendar:
- Regulatory submission deadlines
- Permit renewal dates
- Reporting requirements
- Audit and inspection schedules
- Training certification renewals

**Deliverables:**
- Annual compliance calendar (visual and tabular formats)
- Critical deadlines tracker
- Reminder and escalation procedures

#### Component 3: Risk and Controls Framework (8-10 hours)

**3.1 Compliance Risk Assessment**

Conduct comprehensive compliance risk assessment:
- Identify compliance risks for each obligation category
- Assess inherent risk (likelihood and consequence)
- Document existing controls
- Assess residual risk
- Identify risk treatment priorities

**Deliverables:**
- Compliance risk register (minimum 30 risks)
- Risk heat map visualization
- Top 10 risks detailed assessment
- Risk treatment plan

**3.2 Internal Controls Design**

Design key compliance controls:

| Control Category | Examples |
|------------------|----------|
| Preventive | Policies, training, system controls |
| Detective | Monitoring, audits, inspections |
| Corrective | Incident response, remediation processes |

**For priority risk areas, document:**
- Control objective
- Control description
- Control owner
- Operating frequency
- Testing approach
- Evidence of operation

**Deliverables:**
- Controls matrix (minimum 25 controls)
- Control testing schedule
- Control effectiveness assessment template

#### Component 4: Monitoring and Reporting (5-7 hours)

**4.1 Compliance Monitoring Program**

Design ongoing compliance monitoring:
- Monitoring activities by obligation category
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)
- Reporting frequency and format
- Exception handling procedures

**Deliverables:**
- Monitoring program document
- KPI/KRI framework (minimum 20 indicators)
- Exception reporting procedure

**4.2 Reporting Framework**

Design compliance reporting hierarchy:

| Audience | Report | Frequency | Content Focus |
|----------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Board | Board Compliance Report | Quarterly | Strategic, high-level |
| Executive | Management Dashboard | Monthly | Operational, trend analysis |
| Compliance Committee | Detailed Report | Monthly | Comprehensive, action-focused |
| Operations | Operational Reports | Weekly/Daily | Task-specific |

**Deliverables:**
- Report templates for each audience level
- Sample populated reports with hypothetical data
- Reporting procedure document

#### Component 5: Implementation Roadmap (3-5 hours)

**5.1 Implementation Plan**

Develop phased implementation plan:
- Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Phase 2: Build (Months 4-6)
- Phase 3: Embed (Months 7-12)
- Resource requirements
- Key milestones
- Success criteria

**Deliverables:**
- Implementation project plan
- Resource budget estimate
- Risk assessment for implementation
- Change management considerations

## Capstone B: Regulatory Change Impact Assessment

**Timeline:** Weeks 14-16  
**Estimated Effort:** 25-35 hours  
**Skill Integration:** Regulatory frameworks, stakeholder engagement, risk management, project management

### Executive Summary

Conduct a comprehensive impact assessment for a significant regulatory change affecting WA mining operations. This capstone demonstrates your ability to interpret new legislation, assess organizational impact, and develop implementation plans—skills highly valued during periods of regulatory change.

### Scenario

**Regulatory Change:** Hypothetical amendment to WA environmental regulations introducing new requirements for mine closure planning and progressive rehabilitation

**Key Changes:**
1. Mandatory Mine Closure Plans submitted 5 years before planned closure (previously 2 years)
2. Progressive rehabilitation reporting requirements (annual reporting with specific metrics)
3. Enhanced financial provisioning requirements (increased rehabilitation bonds)
4. Community consultation requirements for closure planning
5. Independent third-party auditing of rehabilitation progress

**Company Profile:**
- Established WA iron ore producer
- Three operating mine sites at different lifecycle stages
- Current closure plans exist but need updating
- Significant rehabilitation liabilities

### Project Components

#### Component 1: Regulatory Analysis (6-8 hours)

**1.1 Detailed Regulatory Interpretation**

Analyze the hypothetical regulatory changes:
- Section-by-section analysis of key provisions
- Comparison with previous requirements
- Identification of ambiguities requiring clarification
- Timeline for implementation

**Deliverables:**
- Regulatory summary document (5-7 pages)
- Requirements comparison table (old vs. new)
- Timeline graphic
- Questions for regulatory clarification

**1.2 Industry Benchmarking**

Research industry response and best practices:
- How similar regulations are implemented in other jurisdictions
- Industry body positions and guidance
- Peer company approaches (based on public information)

**Deliverables:**
- Benchmarking summary
- Best practice recommendations
- Industry guidance compilation

#### Component 2: Organizational Impact Assessment (8-10 hours)

**2.1 Gap Analysis**

Assess current state against new requirements:

| Requirement Area | Current State | Gap | Severity |
|------------------|---------------|-----|----------|
| Closure Planning | Plans exist, 2-year horizon | Need 5-year plans | High |
| Progressive Rehabilitation | Ad-hoc reporting | Formal annual reporting required | High |
| Financial Provisioning | Existing bonds | May need increase | Medium |
| Community Consultation | Limited engagement | Formal process required | High |
| Third-Party Auditing | Internal review only | External audit required | Medium |

**Deliverables:**
- Detailed gap analysis (by site and corporate level)
- Gap severity assessment
- Quick wins vs. longer-term changes

**2.2 Stakeholder Impact Analysis**

Identify impacts across stakeholder groups:
- Operations teams
- Environmental teams
- Finance department
- Community relations
- Legal/compliance
- Board and executive
- External stakeholders (regulators, community, investors)

**Deliverables:**
- Stakeholder impact matrix
- Communication requirements by stakeholder
- Change management considerations

**2.3 Resource and Cost Assessment**

Estimate resources required:
- Internal resource requirements (FTE, time allocation)
- External support (consultants, auditors)
- System/technology needs
- Financial provisioning impacts

**Deliverables:**
- Resource requirements summary
- Cost estimate (order of magnitude)
- Budget impact assessment

#### Component 3: Risk Assessment (5-6 hours)

**3.1 Implementation Risk Assessment**

Identify risks associated with implementing changes:
- Non-compliance risks (penalties, permit impacts)
- Operational risks (resource constraints, competing priorities)
- Financial risks (cost overruns, provisioning impacts)
- Reputational risks (stakeholder perception)

**Deliverables:**
- Implementation risk register
- Risk mitigation strategies
- Contingency planning

**3.2 Compliance Risk Update**

Update compliance risk assessments for new requirements:
- New compliance risks identified
- Changed risk ratings for existing risks
- Control gap assessment

**Deliverables:**
- Updated compliance risk register entries
- Control enhancement recommendations

#### Component 4: Implementation Strategy (6-8 hours)

**4.1 Implementation Roadmap**

Develop phased implementation plan:
- Immediate actions (0-3 months)
- Short-term actions (3-12 months)
- Medium-term actions (12-24 months)
- Long-term actions (24+ months)

**Deliverables:**
- Implementation project plan
- Gantt chart or timeline graphic
- Key milestones and decision points
- Governance structure for implementation

**4.2 Change Management Plan**

Develop change management approach:
- Communication plan
- Training requirements
- Stakeholder engagement activities
- Resistance management

**Deliverables:**
- Change management plan
- Communication schedule
- Training needs assessment

**4.3 Monitoring and Assurance**

Design ongoing assurance approach:
- Implementation progress monitoring
- Compliance verification
- Effectiveness assessment

**Deliverables:**
- Monitoring framework
- Assurance activities calendar
- Reporting requirements

#### Component 5: Executive Reporting (3-4 hours)

**5.1 Board Paper**

Prepare board-ready paper on regulatory change:
- Executive summary
- Key impacts and risks
- Recommended actions
- Resource requirements
- Timeline

**Deliverables:**
- Board paper (3-5 pages plus appendices)
- Board presentation slides (10-12 slides)

**5.2 Management Action Plan**

Prepare actionable management summary:
- Priority actions with owners
- Decision requirements
- Resource requests
- Escalation items

**Deliverables:**
- Management action plan
- Decision paper format summary

## Capstone C: Enterprise Compliance Program Review

**Timeline:** Weeks 16-18  
**Estimated Effort:** 35-45 hours  
**Skill Integration:** Audit, risk management, stakeholder communication, continuous improvement

### Executive Summary

Conduct a comprehensive review of an enterprise compliance program and develop improvement recommendations. This capstone demonstrates your ability to evaluate existing compliance functions and drive continuous improvement—a critical skill for mature compliance roles.

### Scenario

**Company:** Midwest Mining Corporation (hypothetical)  
**Profile:**
- Established WA gold producer, 15 years operating
- Two mine sites, 650 employees
- Mature compliance function (Compliance Manager + 2 Compliance Officers)
- Recent board request for independent assessment of compliance program effectiveness
- No significant compliance failures, but concerns about keeping pace with regulatory changes

**Context:**
- Private equity ownership transition in progress
- Potential ASX listing within 24 months
- Increased stakeholder scrutiny anticipated
- Board seeking assurance on compliance program adequacy

### Project Components

#### Component 1: Assessment Framework (6-8 hours)

**1.1 Develop Assessment Methodology**

Create comprehensive assessment framework based on:
- Australian Standard AS ISO 19600 Compliance Management Systems
- COSO Internal Control Framework
- Industry best practices
- Regulatory expectations

**Assessment Dimensions:**
1. Governance and Culture
2. Risk Assessment
3. Controls
4. Information and Communication
5. Monitoring and Continuous Improvement

**Deliverables:**
- Assessment framework document
- Assessment criteria and rating scales
- Evidence collection guide
- Assessment interview protocols

**1.2 Scope and Planning**

Define assessment scope and approach:
- Areas included/excluded
- Assessment timeline
- Stakeholders to interview
- Documents to review
- Limitations and assumptions

**Deliverables:**
- Assessment scope document
- Assessment plan and timeline
- Document request list
- Interview schedule

#### Component 2: Current State Assessment (10-12 hours)

**2.1 Document Review**

Review and assess compliance documentation:
- Policies and procedures
- Obligations registers
- Risk registers
- Monitoring reports
- Audit reports
- Training records
- Board and committee papers

**For each document category, assess:**
- Completeness
- Currency
- Quality
- Accessibility
- Effectiveness

**Deliverables:**
- Document review summary
- Gap identification
- Quality assessment ratings

**2.2 Process Effectiveness Review**

Assess key compliance processes:
- Obligations identification and tracking
- Risk assessment processes
- Control implementation
- Monitoring activities
- Incident management
- Reporting processes
- Training and awareness

**Deliverables:**
- Process effectiveness assessment
- Process maturity ratings
- Improvement opportunities

**2.3 Stakeholder Perspectives**

Gather stakeholder input (hypothetical interviews):
- Compliance team self-assessment
- Management perception of compliance function
- Operational staff awareness
- Board expectations

**Deliverables:**
- Stakeholder interview summaries
- Perception gap analysis
- Culture assessment observations

#### Component 3: Maturity Assessment (6-8 hours)

**3.1 Compliance Maturity Model**

Assess current maturity against defined model:

| Level | Description | Characteristics |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 - Initial | Ad-hoc compliance | Reactive, individual-dependent |
| 2 - Developing | Basic processes | Documented procedures, some consistency |
| 3 - Defined | Standardized processes | Organization-wide standards, training |
| 4 - Managed | Measured and controlled | KPIs, regular monitoring, data-driven |
| 5 - Optimizing | Continuous improvement | Proactive, predictive, industry-leading |

**Assess maturity across:**
- Each assessment dimension
- Key compliance process areas
- Overall program maturity

**Deliverables:**
- Maturity assessment results
- Maturity radar chart/visualization
- Maturity comparison to target state

**3.2 Benchmarking**

Compare against industry practices:
- Peer company practices (based on public information)
- Industry standards and guidelines
- Regulatory expectations

**Deliverables:**
- Benchmarking summary
- Areas of strength vs. improvement opportunity
- Best practice recommendations

#### Component 4: Findings and Recommendations (8-10 hours)

**4.1 Findings Documentation**

Document assessment findings:
- Strengths and good practices
- Areas for improvement
- Gaps and risks
- Root cause analysis for key issues

**For each finding:**
- Finding ID and description
- Evidence
- Impact assessment
- Root cause
- Recommendation

**Deliverables:**
- Detailed findings register
- Findings by dimension/category
- Priority rating for each finding

**4.2 Improvement Recommendations**

Develop actionable recommendations:
- Quick wins (0-3 months)
- Short-term improvements (3-12 months)

**For each recommendation:**
- Recommendation description
- Expected benefit
- Resource requirements
- Implementation approach
- Success criteria

**Deliverables:**
- Recommendations register
- Prioritized improvement roadmap
- Resource and cost estimates

**4.3 Target Operating Model**

Design future state compliance function:
- Organizational structure recommendations
- Process improvements
- Technology enablers
- Capability development
- Performance management

**Deliverables:**
- Target operating model description
- Transition plan
- Capability development roadmap

#### Component 5: Reporting and Presentation (5-7 hours)

**5.1 Executive Assessment Report**

Prepare comprehensive assessment report:

## Capstone Selection Guide

### Choosing Your Capstone

Consider these factors when selecting which capstone(s) to complete:

| Factor | Capstone A | Capstone B | Capstone C |
|--------|------------|------------|------------|
| **Best for roles in:** | Start-ups, growth companies | Established companies, regulatory affairs | Mature organizations, consulting |
| **Demonstrates:** | Building from scratch | Change management | Assessment and improvement |
| **Complexity:** | High | Medium-High | High |
| **Time required:** | 30-40 hours | 25-35 hours | 35-45 hours |
| **Prerequisites:** | Milestones 1-4 | Milestones 1-3, 5 | Milestones 1-5 |

### Recommended Approach

**Option 1: Complete One Capstone Thoroughly**
- Focus on producing high-quality, detailed deliverables
- Best if time is limited
- Choose based on target role/company type

**Option 2: Complete Two Capstones**
- Demonstrates broader capability range
- Recommended combination: A + B or B + C
- Allow 6-8 weeks total

**Option 3: Complete All Three Capstones**
- Maximum portfolio impact
- Best for serious career changers
- Allow 10-12 weeks total
- Consider doing at reduced scope (key deliverables only)
