# Milestone Projects: Building Your Compliance Portfolio

## Overview

This document contains targeted milestone projects designed to build practical skills and create portfolio artifacts that demonstrate competency to enterprise hiring managers. Each project is based on realistic WA resource sector scenarios and produces tangible deliverables.

**Purpose:** Build progressive skills and portfolio pieces throughout your learning journey  
**Target Audience:** Candidates from non-traditional backgrounds entering compliance/governance roles  
**Portfolio Value:** Each milestone creates artifacts that showcase specific skills hiring managers seek

## Milestone 1: Regulatory Gap Analysis

**Timeline:** Week 3-4 (after completing regulatory framework learning)  
**Estimated Effort:** 8-12 hours  
**Skill Focus:** Regulatory interpretation, gap analysis, compliance assessment

### Scenario

You are a compliance consultant engaged by a junior WA mining company that recently acquired an existing mining operation. The company wants to ensure full compliance with current WA Mining Act and Environmental Protection Act requirements before commencing expanded operations.

### Background

The acquired operation has been running with legacy compliance processes. The new owners have limited documentation of current compliance status. DMIRS has announced an upcoming audit cycle for the region.

### Project Requirements

#### Part 1: Regulatory Requirements Mapping (4 hours)

1. **Research and document key compliance requirements:**
   - WA Mining Act 1978 - tenement conditions and obligations
   - Environmental Protection Act 1986 - clearing permits and environmental management
   - Mines Safety and Inspection Act 1994 - safety management requirements
   - Mining Rehabilitation Fund Act 2012 - rehabilitation obligations

2. **Create a compliance requirements register:**
   - Requirement ID and description
   - Source legislation/regulation
   - Compliance frequency (annual, ongoing, event-triggered)
   - Evidence required
   - Responsible party
   - Consequences of non-compliance

#### Part 2: Gap Analysis Assessment (4-6 hours)

1. **Develop gap analysis methodology:**
   - Assessment criteria
   - Rating scale (Compliant, Partially Compliant, Non-Compliant, Unable to Assess)
   - Evidence collection approach

2. **Create hypothetical assessment findings:**
   - Document 10-15 requirements assessed
   - Identify 3-4 gaps with varying severity
   - Note 2-3 areas of good practice
   - Include evidence observations

#### Part 3: Remediation Roadmap (2-3 hours)

1. **Prioritize identified gaps:**
   - Use risk-based prioritization
   - Consider regulatory deadlines
   - Assess resource requirements

2. **Develop action plan:**
   - Specific remediation actions
   - Responsible parties
   - Target completion dates
   - Success criteria

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Location | Format |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Compliance Requirements Register | `artifacts/compliance-checklists/milestone-01-compliance-requirements-register.md` | Markdown table |
| Gap Analysis Report | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-01-gap-analysis-report.md` | Structured report |
| Remediation Roadmap | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-01-remediation-roadmap.md` | Action plan with timeline |

### Portfolio Value

This milestone demonstrates:
- **Regulatory knowledge:** Understanding of WA resource sector legislation
- **Analytical skills:** Systematic gap identification and assessment
- **Practical application:** Ability to translate regulatory requirements into actionable compliance tasks
- **Risk prioritization:** Business-focused approach to remediation planning

### Talking Points for Interviews

- "I conducted a regulatory gap analysis for a hypothetical mining operation, identifying key compliance requirements across four major pieces of WA legislation."
- "I developed a risk-based remediation roadmap that prioritized actions based on regulatory deadlines and organizational risk exposure."
- "My gap analysis methodology used a structured assessment criteria that could be replicated for ongoing compliance monitoring."

## Milestone 2: Incident Investigation Case Study

**Timeline:** Week 6 (after completing incident reporting learning)  
**Estimated Effort:** 10-15 hours  
**Skill Focus:** Root cause analysis, investigation techniques, documentation

### Scenario

You are the Compliance Officer for a WA gold mining operation. A vehicle rollover incident occurred on site resulting in minor injuries. You must conduct an investigation, identify root causes, and develop preventive measures.

### Background

**Incident Summary:**
- Date: [Current date - 3 days]
- Location: Haul road between pit and processing plant
- Vehicle: Light vehicle (Toyota LandCruiser)
- Injuries: Driver sustained minor bruising, treated on-site
- Damage: Vehicle significant front-end damage, out of service
- Environmental impact: Minor hydrocarbon spill (10L estimated), contained on road surface

**Initial Information:**
- Driver was returning from pit inspection
- Incident occurred at 14:30 on a clear day
- Road conditions were dry
- No other vehicles involved
- Driver has 5 years site experience

### Project Requirements

#### Part 1: Investigation Planning (2 hours)

1. **Develop investigation plan:**
   - Investigation scope and objectives
   - Team composition
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Evidence preservation requirements

2. **Create interview questions:**
   - Driver interview protocol
   - Witness interview protocol
   - Supervisor interview protocol

#### Part 2: Evidence Collection (3-4 hours)

1. **Document hypothetical evidence:**
   - Scene diagram
   - Photographic evidence log
   - Vehicle inspection findings
   - Road condition assessment
   - Training records review
   - Maintenance records review
   - Fatigue management records

2. **Conduct root cause analysis:**
   - Use ICAM (Incident Cause Analysis Method) or similar methodology
   - Identify contributing factors at individual, job, and organizational levels
   - Distinguish immediate causes from underlying causes

#### Part 3: Investigation Report (4-6 hours)

1. **Prepare formal investigation report:**
   - Executive summary
   - Incident description
   - Investigation methodology
   - Findings and root causes
   - Recommendations
   - Appendices

2. **Develop corrective action plan:**
   - SMART actions
   - Responsible parties
   - Completion dates
   - Verification method

#### Part 4: Regulatory Notification (2-3 hours)

1. **Draft DMIRS notification:**
   - Using appropriate notification format
   - Include all required information
   - Meeting notification timeframes

2. **Prepare internal communication:**
   - Safety alert for site personnel
   - Management briefing summary

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Location | Format |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Investigation Plan | `artifacts/case-studies/milestone-02-investigation-plan.md` | Structured plan |
| Root Cause Analysis | `artifacts/case-studies/milestone-02-root-cause-analysis.md` | ICAM diagram and narrative |
| Investigation Report | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-02-incident-investigation-report.md` | Formal report |
| Corrective Action Plan | `artifacts/case-studies/milestone-02-corrective-action-plan.md` | Action tracking table |
| DMIRS Notification Draft | `artifacts/case-studies/milestone-02-dmirs-notification.md` | Notification format |

### Portfolio Value

This milestone demonstrates:
- **Investigation skills:** Structured approach to evidence collection and analysis
- **Root cause analysis:** Understanding of systemic factors contributing to incidents
- **Regulatory awareness:** Knowledge of notification requirements and timelines
- **Communication:** Ability to write professional investigation reports

### Talking Points for Interviews

- "I developed a complete incident investigation package including investigation plan, root cause analysis using ICAM methodology, and formal report."
- "My investigation identified both immediate causes and organizational factors, leading to recommendations that addressed systemic issues."
- "I understand DMIRS notification requirements and can prepare compliant incident reports."

## Milestone 3: Compliance Monitoring Dashboard

**Timeline:** Week 8 (after completing reporting and dashboarding learning)  
**Estimated Effort:** 12-16 hours  
**Skill Focus:** Data visualization, KPI development, compliance reporting

### Scenario

You are tasked with designing a compliance monitoring dashboard for a mid-size WA iron ore mining company with three active mine sites. The executive team wants real-time visibility into compliance status across all operations.

### Background

**Company Profile:**
- 3 active mine sites in the Pilbara region
- 850 employees across all sites
- 12 environmental permits
- Multiple regulatory stakeholders (DMIRS, EPA, DWER)
- Current compliance tracking is spreadsheet-based

**Pain Points:**
- No consolidated view of compliance status
- Reactive rather than proactive compliance management
- Difficulty tracking permit conditions and deadlines
- Limited visibility of leading indicators
- Manual reporting processes consuming significant time

### Project Requirements

#### Part 1: KPI Framework Development (4-5 hours)

1. **Define compliance KPIs:**
   - Leading indicators (predictive measures)
   - Lagging indicators (outcome measures)
   - For each KPI document:
     - KPI name and definition
     - Calculation methodology
     - Data source
     - Target/threshold values
     - Update frequency
     - Owner

2. **KPI Categories to include:**
   - Environmental compliance (permit conditions, exceedances, monitoring)
   - Safety compliance (incident rates, inspections, training)
   - Regulatory compliance (submissions, audits, findings)
   - Permit management (renewals, conditions, amendments)
   - Training compliance (certifications, competencies, refreshers)

#### Part 2: Dashboard Design (5-6 hours)

1. **Create dashboard wireframes:**
   - Executive summary view (single page)
   - Site-level detail view
   - Permit management view
   - Trend analysis view
   - Action tracking view

2. **Design specifications:**
   - Layout and navigation
   - Color coding and visual hierarchy
   - Alert mechanisms
   - Drill-down capabilities
   - Mobile responsiveness considerations

3. **Create mockup using Excel/Google Sheets:**
   - Populate with sample data
   - Include charts and visualizations
   - Demonstrate conditional formatting
   - Show filter functionality

#### Part 3: Implementation Specification (3-5 hours)

1. **Technical requirements document:**
   - Data sources and integration
   - Refresh frequency
   - User access and security
   - Platform recommendations

2. **Business case summary:**
   - Current state challenges
   - Proposed solution benefits
   - Implementation approach
   - Success criteria

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Location | Format |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| KPI Framework Document | `artifacts/dashboards/milestone-03-compliance-kpi-framework.md` | KPI definitions and methodology |
| Dashboard Wireframes | `artifacts/dashboards/milestone-03-dashboard-wireframes.md` | Visual mockups/descriptions |
| Sample Dashboard (Excel) | `artifacts/dashboards/milestone-03-sample-dashboard-description.md` | Description of Excel mockup |
| Implementation Specification | `artifacts/dashboards/milestone-03-implementation-spec.md` | Technical requirements |

### Portfolio Value

This milestone demonstrates:
- **KPI development:** Understanding of meaningful compliance metrics
- **Data visualization:** Ability to design intuitive dashboards
- **Business acumen:** Focus on executive needs and decision support
- **Technical awareness:** Understanding of implementation requirements

### Talking Points for Interviews

- "I designed a comprehensive compliance dashboard framework including 25+ KPIs across environmental, safety, and regulatory categories."
- "My dashboard design focused on providing executives with actionable insights through leading indicators and early warning systems."
- "I developed implementation specifications that considered data integration, security, and ongoing maintenance requirements."

## Milestone 4: Internal Audit Program

**Timeline:** Week 9-10 (integrating multiple learning areas)  
**Estimated Effort:** 15-20 hours  
**Skill Focus:** Audit planning, program development, risk assessment

### Scenario

You are developing an internal compliance audit program for a WA mining company that currently lacks formal internal audit capabilities. The company has experienced increased regulatory scrutiny and wants to proactively identify and address compliance gaps.

### Background

**Company Profile:**
- Mid-size gold mining operation in the Goldfields region
- Single operating mine with processing plant
- 450 employees
- Recently received two regulatory improvement notices
- No dedicated internal audit function

**Management Concerns:**
- Reactive approach to compliance issues
- Inconsistent compliance practices across departments
- Limited assurance of regulatory compliance
- Upcoming DMIRS audit announced

### Project Requirements

#### Part 1: Risk-Based Audit Universe (4-5 hours)

1. **Develop audit universe:**
   - Identify all auditable areas
   - Map to regulatory requirements
   - Assign risk ratings

2. **Create risk assessment methodology:**
   - Risk factors (regulatory, operational, financial, reputational)
   - Rating scales
   - Weighting approach

3. **Prioritize audit areas:**
   - Risk scoring matrix
   - Resource considerations
   - Regulatory priorities

#### Part 2: Annual Audit Plan (4-5 hours)

1. **Develop 12-month audit schedule:**
   - Audit topics and scope
   - Timing and duration
   - Resource requirements
   - Dependencies

2. **Create audit calendar:**
   - Visual representation of audit schedule
   - Key milestones and deadlines
   - Coordination with external audits

#### Part 3: Audit Methodology and Templates (5-6 hours)

1. **Develop audit methodology:**
   - Planning phase procedures
   - Fieldwork procedures
   - Reporting procedures
   - Follow-up procedures

2. **Create audit templates:**
   - Audit charter template
   - Audit plan template
   - Working paper template
   - Finding/observation template
   - Final report template

#### Part 4: Sample Audit Report (3-4 hours)

1. **Conduct mock environmental compliance audit:**
   - Scope: Environmental monitoring obligations
   - Create sample findings
   - Develop recommendations

2. **Prepare sample audit report:**
   - Executive summary
   - Scope and methodology
   - Findings and recommendations
   - Management response section

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Location | Format |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Audit Universe and Risk Assessment | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-04-audit-universe.md` | Risk-rated audit areas |
| Annual Audit Plan | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-04-annual-audit-plan.md` | 12-month schedule |
| Audit Methodology Guide | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-04-audit-methodology.md` | Procedures and standards |

| Sample Audit Report | `artifacts/audit-reports/milestone-04-sample-audit-report.md` | Environmental compliance audit |

### Portfolio Value

This milestone demonstrates:
- **Program development:** Ability to build audit function from scratch
- **Risk-based approach:** Understanding of risk-based audit planning
- **Methodology design:** Creation of repeatable processes and standards
- **Professional standards:** Alignment with internal audit best practices

### Talking Points for Interviews

- "I developed a complete internal audit program including risk-based audit universe, annual plan, and methodology documentation."
- "My audit universe covered 30+ auditable areas with risk ratings based on regulatory, operational, and reputational factors."

## Milestone 5: Stakeholder Communication Package

**Timeline:** Week 11-12 (integrating stakeholder engagement and professional development)  
**Estimated Effort:** 10-15 hours  
**Skill Focus:** Stakeholder communication, regulatory liaison, professional documentation

### Scenario

A WA mining company is responding to a DMIRS inspection that identified several areas requiring improvement. You are responsible for preparing the formal response and developing ongoing communication materials for regulatory stakeholders.

### Background

**Inspection Findings:**
- Three improvement notices issued
- Two advisory notices
- Follow-up inspection scheduled in 90 days
- Company committed to demonstrating improvement

**Stakeholder Context:**
- DMIRS Inspector maintains ongoing relationship
- Community liaison group meets quarterly
- Board requires monthly compliance updates
- Executive team needs progress briefings

### Project Requirements

#### Part 1: Regulatory Response Package (4-5 hours)

1. **Draft formal response letter:**
   - Acknowledgment of findings
   - Commitment to remediation
   - Proposed action plan summary
   - Timeline for completion

2. **Develop detailed action plan:**
   - Actions mapped to each finding
   - Responsible parties
   - Completion dates
   - Evidence to be provided

3. **Create evidence package:**
   - Documentation checklist
   - Progress tracking mechanism
   - Verification approach

#### Part 2: Board Reporting Materials (3-4 hours)

1. **Develop board compliance report:**
   - Compliance status summary
   - Key risks and issues
   - Regulatory relationships
   - Improvement progress

2. **Create executive dashboard summary:**
   - One-page visual summary
   - Traffic light status indicators
   - Key metrics and trends

#### Part 3: Community Communication (3-4 hours)

1. **Prepare community liaison briefing:**
   - Plain language summary of compliance activities
   - Environmental protection measures
   - Community feedback mechanism
   - Contact information

2. **Develop FAQ document:**
   - Common questions about mining operations
   - Environmental and safety commitments
   - Regulatory oversight explanation

#### Part 4: Internal Communication (2-3 hours)

1. **Create employee awareness materials:**
   - Compliance expectations summary
   - Reporting procedures
   - Key contacts

2. **Develop supervisor briefing guide:**
   - Compliance priorities
   - Inspection preparation
   - Documentation requirements

- **Stakeholder management:** Ability to tailor communication to different audiences
