### Key Concepts to Master Today
- **Risk:** Effect of uncertainty on objectives
- **Hazard:** Source of potential harm
- **Likelihood:** Chance of something happening
- **Consequence:** Outcome of an event
- **Risk Appetite:** Amount of risk willing to accept
- **Risk Tolerance:** Acceptable variation in outcomes

### Key Concepts to Master Today
- **Qualitative Assessment:** Uses descriptive scales (low/medium/high)
- **Quantitative Assessment:** Uses numerical values and probabilities
- **Risk Matrix:** Visual tool showing likelihood vs consequence
- **Risk Rating Formula:** Typically Likelihood × Consequence

### Key Risk Register Components
1. **Risk ID:** Unique identifier
2. **Risk Description:** Clear statement of what could go wrong
3. **Category:** Type of risk (operational, compliance, etc.)
4. **Likelihood Rating:** 1-5 scale
5. **Consequence Rating:** 1-5 scale
6. **Risk Rating:** L × C
7. **Risk Owner:** Person responsible
8. **Existing Controls:** What's already in place
9. **Mitigation Actions:** What additional actions needed
10. **Review Date:** When to reassess

## Day 5: Practical Exercise - Create Sample Risk Register

**Time Allocation:** 3-4 hours

### Tasks
- [ ] Select the provided hypothetical mining project scenario
- [ ] Identify at least 10 potential risks across categories
- [ ] Assess each risk using likelihood and consequence scales
- [ ] Document risks in a risk register format
- [ ] Assign risk owners and initial mitigation strategies

### Project Scenario
**Project:** Junior mining company beginning exploration activities in the Pilbara region

**Project Details:**
- Drill program across 3 tenements
- 15 staff including contractors
- Remote camp accommodation
- Heritage-sensitive areas nearby
- Seasonal weather considerations

### Risk Categories to Cover
1. **Occupational Health & Safety** (minimum 2 risks)
2. **Environmental** (minimum 2 risks)
3. **Regulatory/Compliance** (minimum 2 risks)
4. **Operational** (minimum 2 risks)
5. **Financial** (minimum 1 risk)
6. **Community/Stakeholder** (minimum 1 risk)

### Templates to Use
- Risk register template (Excel or Markdown format)
- Risk matrix visualization

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Brainstorm risks for each category
2. Write clear risk descriptions
3. Rate likelihood (1-5) and consequence (1-5)
4. Calculate risk rating
5. Identify existing controls
6. Propose mitigation actions
7. Assign owners
8. Set review dates
