# Week 03: Regulatory Frameworks and WA Legislation

## Overview

**Focus Area:** Understanding WA Resource Sector Legislative Framework  
**Duration:** 1 Week  
**Estimated Time Commitment:** 20-25 hours

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

1. Navigate and interpret WA mining and environmental legislation
2. Understand the regulatory hierarchy (Acts, Regulations, Guidelines, Codes)
3. Identify compliance obligations from legislation
4. Explain the roles of key WA regulatory bodies (DMIRS, EPA, DWER)
5. Apply legislative interpretation techniques to practical scenarios
6. Create compliance requirements registers from regulatory sources
7. Understand approval pathways and licensing requirements
8. Stay current with legislative amendments and updates

## Practical Projects

### Project 1: Compliance Requirements Register (Start of Milestone 1)

**Objective:** Create a comprehensive compliance requirements register for a mining operation

**Scenario:** Gold mining operation transitioning from exploration to production

**Operation Details:**
- Location: Goldfields region, WA
- Current: Exploration License E00/0000
- Proposed: Mining Lease M00/0000
- Annual production: 500,000 tonnes ore, 100,000 oz gold
- Workforce: 85 full-time, 120 contractors (peak)
- Infrastructure: Open pit, processing plant, TSF, accommodation camp
- Water: Groundwater abstraction 500 ML/year
- Known heritage: 3 registered Aboriginal sites nearby

**Requirements:**
1. Identify all applicable WA and Commonwealth legislation
2. Extract specific compliance obligations from each Act/Regulation
3. Categorize by:
   - Approval/licensing requirements
   - Ongoing operational compliance
   - Reporting and notification obligations
   - Monitoring and inspection requirements
   - Record-keeping requirements

4. For each obligation, document:
   - Requirement ID
   - Source (Act, section, regulation)
   - Description of obligation
   - Compliance frequency
   - Evidence required
   - Responsible party/position
   - Consequence of non-compliance
   - Current status (for gap analysis next week)

5. Create at least 40-50 distinct requirements across:
   - Mining Act and Regulations (10-15 requirements)
   - Environmental Protection Act (10-12 requirements)
   - Mines Safety legislation (8-10 requirements)
   - Aboriginal Heritage (3-5 requirements)
   - Water resources (3-5 requirements)
   - Other applicable legislation (5-8 requirements)

**Deliverable Location:** `artifacts/compliance-checklists/milestone-01-compliance-requirements-register.md`

### Project 2: Legislative Interpretation Exercise

**Objective:** Practice interpreting and applying legislative provisions to scenarios

**Tasks:**
1. Select 5 legislative provisions from different Acts
2. For each provision:
   - Explain what it means in plain language
   - Identify who it applies to
   - Describe what actions are required
   - Explain timeframes and frequencies
   - Identify evidence needed to demonstrate compliance
   - Describe consequences of non-compliance

3. Apply each provision to a practical scenario
4. Document interpretation methodology and any ambiguities

**Example Provisions:**
- Mining Act s.74(1A) - Mining Proposal requirements
- EP Act s.51A - Environmental offences
- MSI Act s.13 - Safety management system
- Aboriginal Heritage Act s.18 - Consent to disturb
- Rights in Water Act s.5C - Unlawful taking of water

4. How will I stay current with legislative changes in my career?

### Complexity Management

Legislative research can be overwhelming. Remember:
- Start with overviews and summaries, then dive into detail
- Focus on provisions most relevant to compliance roles
- Use plain language guides and fact sheets
- Build knowledge progressively - you don't need to memorize everything
- Create reference materials you can return to
- Understand where to find information is more important than memorizing it
